# SPAL League Culture

---

## Who we are

The Sergio Parisse Appreciation League is a private fantasy rugby competition run between friends. It has been running for several years, beginning as a spreadsheet and Wix-site operation before moving to a proper app.

The league has a distinct personality: it is lightly absurd, affectionate, and steeped in its own mythology. The app should preserve and reinforce that personality — not sand it away in favour of clean corporate SaaS design.

---

## The Sergio Parisse mythology

The league takes its name from Sergio Parisse, the iconic Italian No. 8 who for many years was the sole reason to watch Italy play in the Six Nations. Selecting an Italian player is not just a rule — it is an act of solidarity, a nod to the league's founding spirit, and a reminder that even the weakest team in the tournament deserves representation.

Parisse himself is retired and no longer plays. The Italian starter rule lives on in his name.

---

## The Italian starter rule

Every manager must start at least one Italian player in their 15. This is non-negotiable by default.

It exists because:
- It honours the league's name and identity.
- It adds strategic constraint — Italy's players are generally cheaper, which creates interesting budget decisions.
- It forces managers to care about Italian fixtures, making the Italy matches feel relevant.
- It is a running source of banter, complaints, and affection in equal measure.

The rule is configurable (it can be disabled by admin if Italy were ever withdrawn from the Six Nations, or if the league voted to change it), but it should be treated as a founding principle, not a minor toggle.

---

## The weakest nation slot

The draft includes a required pick from the weakest nation of the tournament, currently Wales. This slot exists for the same reason as the Italian starter rule: to force managers to engage with the full tournament, not just cherry-pick from the top nations.

The weakest nation is configurable per season. If Wales improves dramatically (or Ireland has a terrible run), the admin can change it.

## Chronicle

The Chronicle is the SPAL equivalent of a league blog. It is where weekly previews, result write-ups, draft recaps, penalty notices, and seasonal reviews live.

The old Wix site carried this function as static blog posts. SPAL should replace that workflow with a proper structured Chronicle module — admin-authored posts with type classification, visibility controls, and round association.

The tone of Chronicle posts should be consistent with the league's voice: informed, irreverent, and occasionally absurd. The app should not force a clinical corporate tone on Chronicle content.

---

## Design tone

SPAL should feel like:

```
personal website
+ private rugby club noticeboard
+ fantasy sports dashboard
+ annual league archive
```

It should not feel like a generic fantasy sports product. Some direction:

- League-specific language throughout (managers, not "users"; The Laws, not "rules"; Chronicle, not "blog").
- Historical archive treated with the same care as current season data.
- Italian and Welsh references woven into the UI where appropriate without being distracting.
- Bold stats, readable tables, and compact position/nation badges.
- Room for personality in empty states, tooltips, and Chronicle content.

---

## History

SPAL has been running since before the spreadsheet era. The app should be capable of archiving all previous seasons — not just displaying them as raw data, but presenting them as proper historical records: champions, final standings, memorable draft picks, notable scores.

The all-time table is important. Managers care about their historical record, not just the current season.
